## Why

Scott Young completed the entire MIT CS curriculum in 12 months using only free materials, no formal enrollment. His post-mortem became one of the most-read pieces in self-directed learning.

I'm doing the same thing with [OSSU](https://github.com/ossu/computer-science) — a structured, free CS curriculum built from university course materials. The difference: I'm documenting everything here, including the wrong turns.

## The Rules

1. No skipping. Every course in the OSSU curriculum, in order.
2. Every week gets an entry. Even the bad ones.
3. All projects ship publicly.
4. No deadline — but consistent forward movement.

## Why Document It

Two reasons. First, writing forces understanding. If I can't explain what `malloc` does, I don't understand `malloc`. Second, accountability — blank weeks are visible.

## What This Blog Is

A log, not a tutorial. Raw notes, code that broke before it worked, concepts explained badly and then better. If it's useful to someone, good. If not, it's still useful to me.
